Our Treatments Include

Acne

Acne i.e. Acne vulgaris, commonly known as a pimple, can exist in various parts of the skin. It can be seen as skin coloured bumps, blackheads, whiteheads, pus filled nodules, or perhaps as deeper cysts.

Acne can either be non-inflammatory or inflammatory lesions, or a mixture of both, and they mostly affect the face but can also be present on the back and chest areas.

Acne predominantly involves blockage and/or inflammation of hair follicles and their accompanying sebaceous (oil) glands.

Pigmentation

Hyperpigmentation is seen as dark spots on the face. This can be due to many reasons: post-acne or any other inflammation, melasma, freckles, etc.

The cause of the pigmentation must be diagnosed and treated accordingly

Hair Fall

If hair loss is due to hormonal imbalances or medical conditions (such as thyroid disorders or polycystic ovary syndrome), treating the underlying condition can often help in preventing further hair loss.

Ensure a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, especially vitamin D, iron, zinc, and biotin. Supplements might be necessary if there are deficiencies.

Dandruff

Dandruff is a common scalp condition characterized by the flaking of dead skin cells from the scalp. It can be accompanied by itching and sometimes redness of the scalp. Dandruff is often caused by various factors

Excessive production of sebum (natural oils) on the scalp can contribute to dandruff. This excess oil can make it easier for the yeast to thrive.

On the other hand, a dry scalp can also lead to flaking and dandruff. Dry skin cells may flake off more easily.

Eczema

Eczema almost always includes itchy skin. The scientific term for itch is “pruritus.” For many people, the itch can range from mild to moderate. Sometimes the itch gets so bad that people scratch it until it bleeds. This is called the “itch-scratch cycle

Choose mild, fragrance-free soaps and detergents. Avoid using harsh or scented products, which can irritate the skin.

Psoriasis

In severe cases, a dermatologist may prescribe systemic medications, which are taken orally or by injection. These medications can be effective in managing psoriasis but may have potential side effects, so regular monitoring is essential.
